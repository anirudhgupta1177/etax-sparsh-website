Brand system

eTax Profilling — Brand Style Guide

A living reference for the people who build and represent eTax Profilling. Consistent logos, colours, type and language keep our brand clear, trustworthy and unmistakably ours — across the site, social, decks and client documents.

Logo

The eTax Profilling mark

Our logo is the gradient checkmark-tick lockup. Use the full lockup wherever space allows; fall back to the standalone mark in tight or square placements like avatars and favicons.

Primary lockup

Full colour

The default. Use on white or very light backgrounds (BG Soft, Primary 50). Always keep the wordmark and mark together at the supplied proportions.

Reversed

On dark

Use the white logo on the ink gradient, navy or photography. Reach for this any time the full-colour mark would lose contrast.

Standalone mark

App icon & favicon

The tick-in-rounded-square mark for avatars, favicons and app tiles where the wordmark won’t fit legibly.

Do
  • Keep clear spaceLeave at least the height of the mark as padding on all sides.
  • Maintain contrastFull colour on light; white logo on dark. Always legible.
  • Scale proportionallyResize from a corner so the mark and wordmark stay in ratio.
Don’t
  • Don’t stretch or squashNever change the aspect ratio or distort the mark.
  • Don’t recolourNo off-brand fills, gradients or drop shadows on the mark.
  • Don’t place on clutterAvoid busy photos or low-contrast backgrounds that reduce legibility.

Typography

Two typefaces, one clear voice

Plus Jakarta Sans handles headings and brand moments (weights 800 / 700 / 600). Inter carries body copy, UI and captions (weights 400 / 500 / 600). The pairing reads as modern, professional and friendly.

Voice & tone

How eTax Profilling sounds

We talk like a trusted, expert friend who happens to be a Chartered Accountant — never a textbook, never a salesperson. Confident, warm and refreshingly plain.

Clear over clever

Say it plainly. Swap jargon for words a first-time filer understands. If a sentence needs a glossary, rewrite it.

Reassuring & expert

Taxes make people anxious. Lead with calm authority — “Relax, we’ve got this” — backed by real CA expertise.

Specific & honest

Use concrete numbers and real benefits, mark savings as illustrative, and never imply government affiliation.

Action-oriented

Every page invites a next step — book a call, see plans, talk to an expert. Verbs first, friction last.

Say this
  • “File your ITR in minutes”Plain, benefit-led, active.
  • “A dedicated CA, not a call centre”Concrete, reassuring, human.
  • “Refund estimates are illustrative”Honest and compliant.
Not this
  • “Leverage our synergistic e-filing ecosystem”Jargon-y and cold.
  • “Guaranteed ₹50,000 refund for everyone”Over-promising and untrue.
  • “Official government tax portal”False affiliation — never use.
